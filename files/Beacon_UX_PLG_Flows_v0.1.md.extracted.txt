1. Approach & Operating Principles
This document speciﬁes the user ﬂows that turn the Beacon product spec into a working PLG
product. Two perspectives are covered:
1. Customer-facing ﬂows — what Mike (owner) and his oﬃce manager experience from ﬁrst
touch through expansion and renewal.
2. Internal admin ﬂows — what the Beacon team (operations, customer success, content
review, billing) experiences running the platform.
Every ﬂow is documented with: goal, entry trigger, step-by-step screen ﬂow, mobile-ﬁrst
wireframe of the critical screen, PLG levers, failure modes and recovery, and the event
instrumentation needed to measure it.

UX principles guiding every screen
1. Two screens. Home + Action Queue. Everything else is sub-views.
2. One number on Home. AI Visibility Score, big, with a 30-day sparkline.
3. No jargon, ever. "How often AI mentions you," not "share of voice."
4. Big tap targets. Mike approves from his phone with grease on his hands.
5. Single-color status. Green (working), yellow (needs attention), red (broken).
6. Mobile-ﬁrst PWA. Native-app feel, web cost.
7. Undo > Conﬁrm. A revert button beats ﬁve conﬁrmation dialogs.

PART I — CUSTOMER-FACING FLOWS

Flow 1 — The Free Audit (PLG Hook)
State transition: Visitor → Audit Completed Goal: Convert anonymous traﬃc to a personalized,
share-worthy result that creates buying intent. Entry trigger: Direct nav to beacon.app, paid ad,
IEC chapter referral, or "audit your AI visibility" CTA from anywhere.
Why this is the most important ﬂow in the product
Without signup, without payment, without a dashboard — the audit produces a real,
personalized AI Visibility Score that the contractor can immediately verify by typing the same
prompts into ChatGPT himself. That veriﬁability is the entire conversion engine. Everything
else ﬂows from this two-minute experience.

Key UX decisions
No "set up your account" multi-page wizard. Everything from the audit pre-ﬁlls. The
signup is a single conﬁrmation screen.
Brand voice samples are auto-collected from public sources. Owner can add private
sources (LinkedIn, ads, voicemails) but nothing is required.
First-ﬁx preview shows the actual content, not a placeholder. This is the credibility
moment. If the FAQ sounds generic, we've lost the customer.
Schema script install is oﬀered, not required. Skipping is ﬁne — the ﬁrst ﬁx can publish to
GBP only. Site ﬁxes can wait until the script is installed.
End-state is the Home screen, not "you're all set!" success page. Drop the user into the
product, not a marketing wrapper.

State: Activated → Habituated Goal: Continuous Score lift + content shipped without owner
attention.
This is not a ﬂow the owner does — it's a ﬂow that happens. The owner's only interaction is the
weekly digest (Flow 4) and approval queue (Flow 5).

2:00 AM   Visibility Radar runs (50 prompts × 3 engines for every active customer)

Edge cases handled silently
LLM API rate limits hit. Queue spills to next day; alert internal ops only if persistent.
Customer's website is down. Skip site-publish actions; queue them for retry. Notify owner
only if down for 48+ hours.
GBP API quota hit. Spread posts across the week; never miss the cadence.
A scheduled publish fails. Auto-retry 3 times over 48h; surface to internal queue if all fail.

Instrumentation Plan Summary
Every event captured in this document maps to one of four buckets:
Bucket Used for
Acquisition Audit funnel, signup, source attribution
Activation Onboarding completion, ﬁrst-ﬁx-shipped, time-to-aha
Engagement Digest opens, queue interactions, login frequency
Conversion / Expansion Upgrade clicks, referrals sent, autopilot enabled
Retention / Churn Cancellation reasons, win-back funnel, save rates
A single event schema (event_name, customer_id, tier, state, properties{}) is used
everywhere. Stored in a warehouse (BigQuery or Snowﬂake) and surfaced via a single dashboard
for the team.
